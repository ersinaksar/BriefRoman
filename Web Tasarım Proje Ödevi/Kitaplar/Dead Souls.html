The main protagonist of the novel is Pavel Ivanovich Chichikov, who stands out for his adventurousness and deception. Being rich is his dream, and he's capable of doing anything to make that dream come true. Pavel travels from town to town in Russia, buying peasants from landowners. But Chichikov has a different purpose; it's just buying slaves who are dead but not recorded. Since every landowner paid taxes for every slave at that time, they think they're making a profit by selling these already dead slaves for money, and they're happy. Pavel, on the other hand, puts hostages on this property that does not exist and receives money in return. It's a very profitable business. First of all, russia's N... he comes to your city. He introduces himself as the landowner. He gains everyone's admiration for his speech and manners. No one suspects him. He meets the elders of the city. Who are important people like the police, the governor, the prosecutor, the judge. He has evening meetings with these people, attends dinners. It creates the impression of a kind and respectful person in people.
