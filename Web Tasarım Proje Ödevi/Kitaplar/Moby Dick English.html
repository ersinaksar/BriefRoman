Ismail is a lonely and lonely man who has spent his life at sea. Being on land didn't give him peace. He was used to the crazy tremors of the ocean. Walking in a flat piece like a curtain, breathing, seemed pointless to him. And he was going to go out to sea again soon to look for work on ships. But this time, the job he wanted was a little different.
He wanted to be a whaling man. At that time, since the light bulb had not yet been invented, candles were used to illuminate the pitch blackness of the night. And, of course, the candle was made of whale oil. Anyway, whaling was one of the hardest jobs. Action at any moment, tension at any time. That's why Ismail wanted to be a whaler.
Ismail, who was planning to stay in an inn on the evening he was staying in Bedford, was able to find a room in a ruined inn, but he had to share it with another man. Ismail, who did not see the man sleeping in the same bed in his darkness, was frightened and screamed when he saw a man of large size with interesting patterns on his face with the shadows hit by the candle. Ismail isn't the only one. He had the same reaction when he saw the guy with the interesting stripes all over his face, Queequeg. Queequeg was used to these reactions. So he was quietly curled up on one end of the bed. Ismail could barely sleep all night.

They all gave up their lives by going after Moby Dick, the legend of the oceans, for revenge.
